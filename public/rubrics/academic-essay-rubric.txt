ACADEMIC ESSAY ASSESSMENT RUBRIC
Undergraduate Level · Argumentative/Expository Writing
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. THESIS & CONTENT (25 points)
───────────────────────────────
25-22  Excellent: Clear, arguable thesis. Demonstrates deep understanding of the topic. All content directly supports the central argument. Original insights present.
21-18  Good: Clear thesis with adequate depth. Most content is relevant and well-developed. Some original thinking evident.
17-13  Developing: Thesis is vague or overly broad. Content is relevant but lacks depth or specificity. Limited analysis beyond surface-level observations.
12-0   Inadequate: No clear thesis. Content is off-topic, superficial, or largely unsupported. Little evidence of engagement with the topic.

2. ARGUMENTATION & EVIDENCE (25 points)
───────────────────────────────────────
25-22  Excellent: Claims are well-supported with 2+ premises each. Warrants explicitly connect evidence to claims. Counter-arguments are acknowledged and addressed. No logical fallacies.
21-18  Good: Most claims have supporting evidence. Some warrants present. At least one counter-argument acknowledged. Minor reasoning gaps.
17-13  Developing: Several claims lack evidence. Warrants are mostly absent. No counter-arguments addressed. Some unsupported assertions.
12-0   Inadequate: Most claims are unsupported opinions. No engagement with opposing views. Frequent logical fallacies or circular reasoning.

3. ORGANIZATION & COHERENCE (20 points)
───────────────────────────────────────
20-18  Excellent: Clear introduction–body–conclusion structure. Smooth paragraph transitions. Each paragraph has a clear purpose. Ideas flow logically from one to the next. Introduction and conclusion are aligned.
17-14  Good: Adequate structure with minor organizational issues. Most transitions are effective. Occasional lapses in paragraph focus.
13-10  Developing: Structure is present but uneven. Some paragraphs lack focus or topic sentences. Abrupt transitions. Topic drift evident in places.
9-0    Inadequate: No discernible organizational structure. Paragraphs are disjointed. No clear introduction or conclusion. Significant topic drift.

4. LANGUAGE & ACADEMIC REGISTER (15 points)
───────────────────────────────────────────
15-13  Excellent: Sophisticated, varied vocabulary appropriate to academic context. Good use of discipline-specific terminology. Sentences vary in length and structure. Appropriate hedging and boosting. Formal, impersonal tone maintained.
12-10  Good: Generally appropriate academic vocabulary. Some sentence variety. Mostly formal tone with minor lapses. Adequate hedging.
9-7    Developing: Limited vocabulary range. Repetitive sentence structures. Informal tone intrudes. Over-reliance on simple sentences. Hedging or boosting imbalance.
6-0    Inadequate: Vocabulary is basic or inappropriate. Sentences are uniformly simple. Informal or conversational tone throughout. No awareness of academic register.

5. COHESION & REFERENTIAL CLARITY (10 points)
──────────────────────────────────────────────
10-9   Excellent: Entities are clearly introduced and consistently referenced throughout. Pronouns have clear antecedents. Lexical chains maintain topic threads across paragraphs. Connectives effectively signal relationships.
8-7    Good: Generally clear referencing with occasional ambiguity. Most entities are maintained. Adequate use of connectives.
6-4    Developing: Frequent unclear pronoun references. Entities introduced but not sustained. Limited use of connectives. Reader must infer connections.
3-0    Inadequate: Persistent referential ambiguity. Entities appear and disappear. No connective use. Text reads as disconnected sentences.

6. CRITICAL THINKING & DEPTH (5 points)
────────────────────────────────────────
5      Excellent: Demonstrates nuanced understanding. Considers multiple perspectives. Identifies implications and limitations. Moves beyond description to analysis and evaluation.
4      Good: Shows analytical thinking. Considers at least two perspectives. Some evaluation beyond description.
3-2    Developing: Mostly descriptive. Limited analysis. Single perspective dominates. Few implications discussed.
1-0    Inadequate: Entirely descriptive or opinion-based. No analytical depth. No consideration of alternative viewpoints.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
TOTAL: 100 points

Grading Scale:
  A   90-100    Outstanding
  A-  85-89     Excellent
  B+  80-84     Very Good
  B   75-79     Good
  B-  70-74     Above Average
  C+  65-69     Average
  C   60-64     Below Average
  D   50-59     Poor
  F   0-49      Failing

METRIC-TO-CRITERION ALIGNMENT NOTES (for instructors)

This rubric is designed to align with Neo-Coh-Metrix analysis layers:

  Criterion 1 (Thesis & Content)
    → L4 Semantic Cohesion (topic coherence, on-topic score)
    → L5 Situation Model (causal depth, temporal grounding)

  Criterion 2 (Argumentation & Evidence)
    → L7 Argumentation (claims, premises/claim, rebuttals)
    → L6 Rhetorical Structure (evidence relations)
    → L8 Pragmatic Stance (evidentiality score)

  Criterion 3 (Organization & Coherence)
    → L0 Surface (paragraph structure, intro/body/conclusion ratio)
    → L4 Semantic Cohesion (topic drift, paragraph cohesion, intro-conclusion alignment)
    → L6 Rhetorical Structure (tree depth, diversity)

  Criterion 4 (Language & Academic Register)
    → L1 Lexical Sophistication (surprisal, AWL density, AoA)
    → L2 Syntactic Complexity (MDD, subordination, NP elaboration)
    → L8 Pragmatic Stance (hedging, hedge-to-boost ratio)

  Criterion 5 (Cohesion & Referential Clarity)
    → L3 Referential Cohesion (argument overlap, coreference chains)
    → L4 Semantic Cohesion (local overlap, coherence variance)

  Criterion 6 (Critical Thinking & Depth)
    → L7 Argumentation (counter-arguments, argument depth)
    → L5 Situation Model (causal chain length)
    → L6 Rhetorical Structure (contrast/concession ratio)
